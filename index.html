The Problem

The joy of baking is lost in the friction of fine motor demands.

Baking requires a demanding combination of gross motor strength (pouring heavy ingredients) and fine motor precision (measuring spices, leveling spoons). For the millions of people living with arthritis, Parkinson’s, or similar mobility issues, the fine motor requirements are often an insurmountable barrier.

Furthermore, managing these physical challenges simultaneously with complex recipe steps creates Cognitive-Motor Interference (CMI). Research shows that attempting to 'dual-task'—managing physical tremors while recalling instructions—significantly increases error rates and safety risks in the kitchen.

Our Solution

Measurely bridges the gap between physical limitations and baking perfection.

Measurely is an assistive baking device designed to restore independence in the kitchen. It combines an automated dispenser that handles the difficult, precise measurements of small ingredients, with an integrated scale and guided display that simplifies the measuring of large ingredients.

By automating the fine-motor tasks (like measuring salt) and guiding the gross-motor tasks (like pouring flour), Measurely allows users to bake with confidence, consistency, and precision.

Our Process

Designed for real people and refined with real feedback.

Measurely was created through extensive research and user testing with real bakers. We observed the pain points, confusing interactions, and moments of frustration that arise when managing the physical and cognitive demands of baking to iterate on our design.
